# v1.1.0-beta Communication Schedule

**Purpose**: Coordinate all communications during the beta release cycle
**Timeline**: January 10 - February 1, 2026
**Target Audiences**: Beta testers, community, team

---

## Communication Principles

1. **Transparency**: Clear about what's being tested and why
2. **Timeliness**: Right information at the right time
3. **Clarity**: Avoid jargon, explain technical concepts
4. **Consistency**: Same message across all channels
5. **Responsiveness**: Quick turnaround on questions

### Escalation Paths

**P0 (Critical - Privacy leak, security, data loss)**:
- Immediate notification: Release Manager + Engineering Lead
- Communication: Within 1 hour of discovery
- Use: Incident response plan (`v1.1.0-beta-incident-response-plan.md`)

**P1 (High - Major bugs)**:
- Notification: Release Manager within 4 hours
- Communication: Within 24 hours to beta testers if affects testing

**P2-P3 (Medium-Low)**:
- Track in GitHub issues
- Weekly summary to team
- Address in v1.1.1

## Contingency Plans

### Scenario 1: Low Recruitment Response (<10 applicants by Jan 11)
**Action**:
- Extend deadline by 1 day (Jan 13 â†’ Jan 14 start)
- Reach out to backup list (10-15 more people)
- Post on additional channels (Reddit, Hacker News)
- Lower bar to 3 testers minimum (vs ideal 5)

### Scenario 2: High Dropout Rate (>50% testers inactive after Day 2)
**Action**:
- Personal check-in calls/emails
- Identify blockers (technical issues, time constraints)
- Extend beta by 2 days if needed (Jan 17 â†’ Jan 19)
- Recruit from waitlist if testers formally drop out

### Scenario 3: Critical Bug Discovery (P0 during beta)
**Action**:
- Immediate notification to all beta testers (stop testing)
- Emergency hotfix (within 4 hours)
- Re-test with testers (1 day extension)
- Document in incident response log

### Scenario 4: Privacy Leak Reported
**Action**:
- STOP all testing immediately
- Activate incident response plan (P0 severity)
- Notify all testers within 1 hour
- Investigate and fix validation (emergency priority)
- Manual review of all exports before resuming
- Delay release until verified fix

### Scenario 5: NO-GO Decision (Jan 23)
**Action**:
- Communicate to beta testers (delay notification)
- Post delay announcement on GitHub (with transparency)
- Revised timeline communication (within 24 hours)
- Weekly updates until GO decision

## Communication Audit Checklist

Before sending any communication:

- [ ] Audience correct (beta testers vs community vs team)
- [ ] Timing appropriate (morning for action items, evening for summaries)
- [ ] Tone matches brand (helpful, transparent, respectful)
- [ ] Call-to-action clear (what do they need to do?)
- [ ] Links working (test all URLs)
- [ ] Grammar/spelling checked
- [ ] Consistent with previous messaging
- [ ] Privacy-sensitive content reviewed (no accidental PII)
- [ ] Approval obtained (if required for public announcements)

---

## Post-Beta Communication Review

After beta completion, review:

1. **Response Rates**:
   - Email open rates
   - Survey completion rate
   - Question/issue volume

2. **Timing**:
   - Were reminders sent at right times?
   - Did testers have enough notice?
   - Was communication too frequent or too sparse?

3. **Clarity**:
   - What questions were asked repeatedly? (documentation gap)
   - What caused confusion? (unclear messaging)
   - What worked well? (repeat for v1.1.1)

4. **Effectiveness**:
   - Did beta testers complete all tasks?
   - Was feedback quality high?
   - Were critical issues surfaced early?
