## Purpose

This document defines the capacity planning and resource management strategy for v1.1.0-beta release, ensuring sustainable team workload, preventing burnout, and allocating resources effectively across all release activities from planning through post-launch stabilization.

---

## Audience

- **Primary**: Release Manager, Team Leads
- **Secondary**: Full team (for workload awareness)
- **Tertiary**: Future project managers

---

## Core Principles

1. **Sustainable Pace**: Marathon, not sprint - avoid burnout
2. **Buffer Time**: Plan for 70% capacity (30% buffer for unknowns)
3. **Clear Priorities**: Focus on critical path, defer non-essentials
4. **Flex Capacity**: Some tasks can slip, some cannot
5. **Team Wellness**: Health and morale > hitting arbitrary deadlines

## Section 4: Risk Management & Contingency

### Capacity Risks

**Risk 1: Team Member Unavailable**
- **Probability**: Medium (illness, emergency)
- **Impact**: High if critical path owner
- **Mitigation**:
  - Cross-train team members
  - Document runbooks
  - Identify backup owners
  - Keep buffer capacity (30%)

**Risk 2: Tasks Take Longer Than Estimated**
- **Probability**: High (planning is hard)
- **Impact**: Medium (schedule slip)
- **Mitigation**:
  - Conservative estimates (multiply by 1.5x)
  - Buffer capacity (30%)
  - Descope P2/P3 tasks if needed
  - Adjust launch date if critical

**Risk 3: Scope Creep**
- **Probability**: Medium (new ideas emerge)
- **Impact**: High (schedule slip, quality reduction)
- **Mitigation**:
  - Strict scope management
  - Release Manager as gatekeeper
  - Defer to v1.2.0 by default
  - Require unanimous team agreement for scope additions

**Risk 4: Critical Bug Found Late**
- **Probability**: Low (extensive testing)
- **Impact**: Critical (launch delay or rollback)
- **Mitigation**:
  - Beta testing (3-5 testers, 3 days)
  - Comprehensive test suite
  - Reserve capacity for bug fixes
  - Rollback plan ready

---

---

### Burnout Prevention

**Warning Signs**:
- Working late nights regularly (>10 PM)
- Working weekends without breaks
- Declining code quality or communication
- Frustration or negative sentiment
- Physical symptoms (fatigue, headaches)

**Preventive Measures**:
- Limit "on-call" hours (max 8 hours/day during launch)
- Mandatory breaks (lunch, every 2 hours)
- No mandatory weekend work (except P0 emergencies)
- Celebrate small wins daily
- Check-in on team morale weekly

**Intervention**:
- If burnout signs detected:
  1. Talk to team member privately
  2. Reduce workload immediately
  3. Extend timeline if needed
  4. Adjust expectations

**Remember**: Team health > hitting dates

## Section 9: Post-Launch Capacity Planning

### Transition to Steady State

**Week 1 Post-Launch** (T+1 to T+7):
- Capacity: 100% for first 3 days, 75% for days 4-7
- Focus: Stabilization, bug fixes, user support
- Intensive monitoring and rapid response

**Week 2-4 Post-Launch** (T+8 to T+30):
- Capacity: 50% (return to normal)
- Focus: Remaining bugs, feedback integration, planning v1.2.0
- Reduced intensity, sustainable pace

**Steady State** (T+30+):
- Capacity: 25% (1 day/week per person)
- Focus: Maintenance, community, next features
- Long-term sustainable pace
