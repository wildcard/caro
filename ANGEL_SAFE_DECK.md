### What is a SAFE?

**SAFE = Simple Agreement for Future Equity**

Created by Y Combinator, it's the standard for early-stage startups:
- Not a loan (no interest, no repayment)
- Not equity (no board seat, no voting rights... yet)
- Converts to equity at next priced round

**How it works:**
```
1. You invest $50K today via SAFE
2. Company continues operating
3. Series Seed happens ($1.5M @ $12M pre)
4. Your SAFE converts to equity:
   - 20% discount on $12M = $9.6M effective
   - $50K / $9.6M = 0.52% of company
   - But valuation cap protects you if price is higher
```

**Why SAFE > Equity:**
- Fast (no valuation negotiation)
- Simple (3-page document)
- Founder-friendly (no board seat yet)
- Investor-friendly (cap + discount protection)

**Q: Why SAFE instead of priced equity round?**
A: SAFEs are faster (no valuation negotiation), simpler (3 pages), and standard for pre-seed. You get the same upside with less friction.

**Q: What if you never raise a priced round?**
A: SAFE converts on acquisition too. If we sell for $50M before Series Seed, your SAFE converts at the $4M cap (1.25% ownership).

**Q: Can I invest from my IRA/401(k)?**
A: Yes, through a self-directed IRA. We can provide resources. Consult your tax advisor.

**Q: Are there tax benefits?**
A: Potentially (QSBS - Qualified Small Business Stock). If you hold for 5+ years, up to $10M gain is tax-free. Consult your CPA.

**Q: What's the risk of dilution?**
A: Every round dilutes everyone. Pro-rata rights let you maintain ownership by investing more. Example path:
- Seed: You own 1.25%
- Series A: Diluted to 0.85% (can invest more to maintain)
- Series B: Diluted to 0.60%
- Exit: Still significant return on $50K

**Q: How do I exit / sell my shares?**
A: Illiquid until company exit (acquisition or IPO). Some secondary sales possible in later rounds (we facilitate). Plan to hold 5-7 years.

**Q: What if the company fails?**
A: SAFE is last in line (after debt, employees, customers). You lose your investment. This is why it's high-risk, high-reward. Only invest what you can afford to lose.

**Q: Can I invest as a company/entity?**
A: Yes (LLC, corporation, fund). We'll need entity docs.

**Q: Do you have a minimum fundraise amount?**
A: No minimum. We operate even with $100K raised (extends runway 12 months). More is better, but we're capital-efficient.

**Q: Who else is investing?**
A: [List notable angels if committed]. We'll share full cap table after you sign.

**Q: What happens to my investment if founders leave?**
