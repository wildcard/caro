## ‚ú® Our Standards

### Expected Behavior

We expect all community members to:

**Be Respectful**
- Treat everyone with dignity and consideration
- Use welcoming and inclusive language
- Respect differing viewpoints and experiences
- Accept constructive criticism gracefully
- Focus on what's best for the community

**Be Helpful**
- Answer questions patiently and thoroughly
- Share knowledge and resources freely
- Celebrate others' successes
- Provide constructive, actionable feedback
- Mentor newcomers

**Be Professional**
- Use appropriate language in all communications
- Stay on-topic in discussions
- Attribute credit where due
- Disclose conflicts of interest
- Respect others' time and attention

**Be Collaborative**
- Seek consensus and compromise
- Listen actively and empathetically
- Assume good intentions
- Ask questions before making accusations
- Work together to resolve conflicts

**Be Transparent**
- Communicate openly and honestly
- Admit mistakes and learn from them
- Share decision-making rationale
- Document important discussions
- Keep the community informed

**Harassment & Discrimination**
- ‚ùå Offensive comments related to protected characteristics

- ‚ùå Sustained disruption of discussions

- ‚ùå Impersonating project members or organizations
- ‚ùå Plagiarism or claiming others' work as your own
- ‚ùå Violating software licenses
- ‚ùå Attempting to manipulate community decisions

**Technical Misconduct**
- ‚ùå Malicious code in contributions
- ‚ùå Intentional security vulnerabilities
- ‚ùå Sabotaging others' contributions
- ‚ùå Abuse of maintainer privileges
- ‚ùå Coordinated disruption of project infrastructure

## üõ°Ô∏è Reporting & Enforcement

### How to Report

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior:

**1. For immediate safety concerns:**
Contact project maintainers directly via email (to be established)

**2. For less urgent issues:**
- Use GitHub's reporting features
- Message a maintainer on Discord
- Email the conduct team (to be established)

**3. For public discussions gone wrong:**
Flag the content and tag a moderator

**All reports will be:**
- Handled confidentially
- Reviewed by multiple people
- Investigated thoroughly
- Responded to within 48 hours

---

### What Happens Next

**We will:**
1. Acknowledge your report within 24 hours
2. Investigate the situation thoroughly
3. Protect reporters from retaliation
4. Keep you informed of actions taken
5. Respect your privacy

**We won't:**
- Dismiss reports without investigation
- Publicly disclose reporter identity without consent
- Retaliate against good-faith reporters
- Ignore patterns of behavior

---

### Enforcement Actions

Depending on the severity and frequency of violations:

**First Offense (Minor):**
- **Action:** Warning and explanation
- **Example:** Unintentional use of exclusionary language
- **Outcome:** Opportunity to correct behavior

**Second Offense (Moderate):**
- **Action:** Temporary suspension (7-30 days)
- **Example:** Repeated disrespect after warning
- **Outcome:** Required reflection period

**Third Offense or Severe Violation:**
- **Action:** Permanent ban from community
- **Example:** Harassment, threats, malicious code
- **Outcome:** Removal from all community spaces

**Appeal Process:**
- Contact conduct team within 30 days
- Provide context and demonstrate understanding
- Accept that decisions may be final

## ü§ù Contribution Guidelines

### For New Contributors

**Welcome!** We're excited you're here.

**Getting started:**
1. Read [CONTRIBUTING.md](../CONTRIBUTING.md)
2. Check [good first issue](https://github.com/wildcard/cmdai/labels/good%20first%20issue) label
3. Ask questions in #help channel
4. Start small (docs, tests, small bugs)
5. Learn our workflow before tackling big features

**You don't need to:**
- Be an expert in Rust
- Know the entire codebase
- Get permission to start working
- Be perfect on your first try

**You should:**
- Communicate early and often
- Ask for help when stuck
- Accept feedback gracefully
- Test your changes thoroughly
- Update documentation

---

### For Maintainers

**Your responsibilities:**
- Review PRs within 1 week
- Provide constructive feedback
- Merge or reject with clear explanation
- Mentor new contributors
- Model community values

**When reviewing:**
- Assume good intentions
- Focus on code, not coder
- Explain the "why" behind feedback
- Celebrate what's done well
- Make it a learning experience

**When rejecting:**
- Explain clearly and kindly
- Suggest alternatives if possible
- Offer to help them understand
- Close with encouragement

---

### For Everyone

**Good contributions:**
- Solve real problems
- Include tests
- Update documentation
- Follow code style (clippy, rustfmt)
- Consider safety implications

**Bad contributions:**
- Lack tests or documentation
- Ignore feedback
- Break existing functionality
- Introduce security issues
- Copy code without attribution

## üéì Best Practices

### Asking for Help

**Good question:**
```
I'm trying to configure cmdai to use Ollama backend, but I'm getting
a connection error. Here's my config:

[backend]
primary = "ollama"
[backend.ollama]
base_url = "http://localhost:11434"

And the error:
"connection refused at localhost:11434"

I've verified Ollama is running with `ollama list`. Any ideas?
```

**Needs improvement:**
```
cmdai doesn't work help!!!
```

**Template:**
1. What you're trying to do
2. What you tried
3. What happened instead
4. Relevant config/error messages
5. Your environment (OS, cmdai version)

---

### Providing Feedback

**Good feedback:**
```
This approach works, but I'm concerned about performance with large files.
Have you considered using a streaming approach instead?

Example: [code snippet or link]

What do you think?
```

**Needs improvement:**
```
This code is slow. Rewrite it.
```

**Template:**
1. Acknowledge what's working
2. Explain the concern
3. Suggest an alternative
4. Ask, don't demand

---

### Disagreeing Respectfully

**Good disagreement:**
```
I see your point about [X], but I'm concerned that [Y]. Here's why...

I think we should consider [alternative approach] because [reasoning].

What am I missing?
```

**Needs improvement:**
```
That's wrong. You clearly don't understand [X].
```

**Template:**
1. Acknowledge their perspective
2. State your concern
3. Propose alternative
4. Invite dialogue
5. Assume you might be wrong
