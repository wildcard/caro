## Summary of Changes

This document refines Caro's launch positioning from **general developer tool** to
**specialized sub-agent for DevOps/SREs**. The core product remains the same;
the messaging becomes more focused.

## Refined Core Messaging

### North Star Statement

> "Caro is the local-first shell companion for DevOps/SREs who refuse to sacrifice
> privacy for productivity. It suggests the shell command you forgot—offline,
> with explicit confirmation, and transparent safety rules. No cloud. No data.
> No excuses."

### Key Positioning Anchors (Non-Negotiable)

1. **Local-First Philosophy**
   - "No cloud. No data collection. No telemetry."
   - Not "privacy-focused" (too vague)—be explicit

2. **Safety Over Convenience**
   - "Rule-based checks + explicit confirmation every time"
   - Don't trust AI safety claims—validate independently

3. **Intentionally Focused**
   - "A specialized sub-agent, not a full AI replacement"
   - We do shell commands. That's it.

4. **For Terminal Professionals**
   - DevOps/SREs/Sysadmins specifically
   - Not "developers" (too broad)

## Pain Point-First Messaging

### The Story (Use in all channels)

```
You're debugging a production issue at 2am. You need to find log files
modified in the last hour, but your brain blanks on the exact find syntax.
You open a browser tab. Stack Overflow. Copy. Paste. Typo. Google again.

The context switch just cost you 5 minutes and your flow state.
```

### Why This Works

1. **Specific and relatable** - SREs have lived this
2. **Shows the problem clearly** - Context-switching, not "productivity"
3. **Sets up the solution** - Quick recall, not AI everything
4. **Avoids hype** - Just describes reality

## Audience Refinement

### Primary: DevOps Engineers & SREs
- **Job:** Manage infrastructure, respond to incidents, automate operations
- **Pain:** Forgotten command syntax during critical moments
- **Context:** Often on production servers, need reliability and safety
- **Why Caro:** Offline capability, safety checks, focused tooling

### Secondary: System Administrators
- **Job:** Maintain systems, manage users, handle backups
- **Pain:** Infrequent complex commands (tar, find, sed combinations)
- **Context:** May work on air-gapped or restricted networks
- **Why Caro:** No cloud dependency, explicit confirmation

### Tertiary: Infrastructure Engineers
- **Job:** Build and maintain infrastructure tooling
- **Pain:** Context-switching for shell syntax details
- **Context:** Care about privacy and tool philosophy
- **Why Caro:** Open source, local-first, focused design

### Who We're NOT For (Be Clear)

- General developers who want AI coding assistance → Copilot, OpenCode
- Users who need the most capable model → Cloud tools
- Teams needing collaboration features → Enterprise tools
- Users who want a new terminal experience → Warp, Fig

## Competitive Positioning Summary

### Our Lane

| We ARE | We ARE NOT |
|--------|------------|
| Shell command recall tool | General AI coding assistant |
| Local-first by philosophy | Cloud with local option |
| Rule-based safety validation | AI-powered safety claims |
| For DevOps/SREs specifically | For all developers |
| Intentionally focused | Feature-rich platform |

### When Competitors Come Up

**Frame responses around YOUR strengths, not their weaknesses:**

- OpenCode more general? → "Yes, we're specialized for shell commands"
- Raycast more features? → "Yes, we're intentionally focused"
- Copilot bigger player? → "Yes, we're privacy-first and offline-only"
- ChatGPT more capable? → "Yes, we're optimizing for speed and privacy"

## Messaging Checklist
